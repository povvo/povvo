# Visual Design DNA

## Visual Design DNA

## Core Visual Territory

Named territories: off-white spec sheet, black identity board, optical scan aperture, construction drawing, halftone crop texture, and product/diagram split. The repeated grammar is a measured field interrupted by one compressed anchor and small instrument marks.

## Dominant Emotional Register

Quiet inspection pressure: cool, exact, high-contrast, and slightly tense at crop edges.

## Repeated Grammar

Measured fields repeat as off-white or black surfaces. Black anchors repeat as compressed type or solid boards. Cyan repeats as line, focus, scan, and rare accent. Registration marks, slashes, vector nodes, and ticks repeat as the motif set.

## Invariants

Black/off-white dominance, cyan subordination, readable body type, active silence, bounded product material, no people or sport subject matter.

## Allowed Variation

Field versus board, sparse versus dense burst, static versus scan, product photo versus line drawing.

## Contradictions To Preserve

Flat field versus optical aperture; sparse silence versus dense burst; finished mark versus visible construction.

## Anti-Patterns

Large saturated cyan, generic dark UI, copied source subjects, soft lifestyle imagery, and product glass as default.

Povvo Spec-Scan is a measured graphic language centered on one canonical identity system: the interrupted-aperture P integrated into the POVVO wordmark and detached as the standalone mark. Warm off-white and ink black carry the field; pale cyan exposes outline construction, ruler ticks, registration marks, vector nodes, and scan edges. The wordmark and P use matched filled and outline states. No unrelated symbol or modular shape may replace the P. Active silence, low print grain, and controlled optical falloff complete the language. People, sport subject matter, product imagery, and generic futuristic interfaces are excluded.

Operational terms covered here: Core visual territory, Repeated grammar, Anti-patterns. These terms are used as build rules, not placeholders. Every artifact should be reconstructable without reopening proof material: choose a measured field or black board, place the integrated POVVO wordmark or standalone interrupted-aperture P as the identity anchor, expose its matched outline construction only where useful, and add only measured marks needed for alignment or state.

## Rules

- Black and off-white carry the system's visible weight.
- Muted cyan identifies instrument lines, focus brackets, scan falloff, and rare accents.
- Dense information appears as a bounded burst, then returns to a quiet field.
- Display type can crop and compress; body and state text stay readable.
- Source subjects are not reused; the language is standalone.

## Drift Controls

Fidelity check: preserve the interrupted-aperture P, identical integrated and standalone geometry, matched filled and outline POVVO states, black and off-white dominance, pale cyan hairlines, tiny measured marks, active negative space, scan or bracket state logic, and exact text control. Reject alternate logo geometry, unrelated symbols, modular blobs, saturated cyan panels, glossy control-room styling, generic cards, product or lifestyle imagery, people-led subjects, sport content, copied source marks, and extra generated words.
